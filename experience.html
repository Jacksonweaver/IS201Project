Experience

This is what I have done.

In the heart of the Andes Mountains, on a frozen lake surrounded by towering peaks, I found myself locked in a life-or-death struggle with a wild boar and a stealthy puma. With adrenaline coursing through my veins, I prepared to face these formidable adversaries armed only with my bare fists. As the wild boar charged towards me, I maneuvered skillfully to avoid its tusks. With a calculated countermove, I redirected the boar's charge towards the approaching puma, creating a chaotic collision on the slippery ice.

Seizing the opportunity, I swiftly engaged the dazed puma, locking it in a tight headlock. Despite the puma's fierce resistance, I maintained control and gradually subdued the predator. With a final effort, the puma succumbed to unconsciousness, its body going limp in my grasp. Meanwhile, the ice beneath us groaned and cracked under the weight of our struggle. As I released the unconscious puma, I turned to confront the wild boar, which had recovered and was preparing for another attack.

With adrenaline-fueled determination, I engaged the boar in a fierce struggle. The ice beneath us continued to fracture, creating a precarious and unstable battleground. As the boar fought desperately, I landed a decisive blow that incapacitated the beast. Suddenly, with a resounding crack, the ice gave way beneath us. The wild boar, mortally wounded, sank into the frigid waters below, disappearing from sight. I scrambled to solid ground, narrowly escaping the treacherous collapse of the icy lake.

The fierce battle had taken its toll, but I had emerged victorious against nature's relentless trials. Gathering my strength, I continued my journey through the majestic Andes, the memory of this epic confrontation etched into my mind. Each step forward was a testament to the resilience and fortitude required to survive amidst the breathtaking yet unforgiving wilderness of the mountains.

As I grappled with the wild puma in the Andes, locking it into a headlock amidst the icy battleground, I drew upon every ounce of strength and determination. Wrestling with nature's predator taught me resilience and adaptability, qualities that would serve me well upon my return to Brigham Young University (BYU) for my sophomore year. Back on campus, during a spirited event featuring BYU's mascot, Cosmo the Cougar, an unexpected turn of events led to a whimsical yet intense showdown.

As Cosmo playfully pounced towards me, his spirited demeanor suddenly turned fierce, as if challenging me to a true test of strength. With the crowd cheering and adrenaline surging, I matched Cosmo's energy and engaged in a mock wrestling match that quickly escalated into a thrilling display of athleticism.

I saw flashbacks pass through my minds eye as I felt Cosmo's attitude change; he sought the same as that puma on the ice those many months ago. As we went back and forth trading strikes, I realized what I had to do. Finally, after dodging a swift left paw, I swung behind him up onto his back, and just as I had before, threw my right arm around his neck, locking it with my other. He struggled for a minute, wickedly writhing to escape my grasp, but he could not. Unconscious, he sunk to the ground, and as I released him he crashed to the ground, face down. I was victorious. I would be the new mascot of Brigham Young University.
